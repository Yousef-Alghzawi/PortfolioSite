Common Questions

FAQ

Do you offer authorship or acknowledgment?

My role is typically acknowledged in the methods section or author list depending on the level of contribution, per ICMJE guidelines. We discuss this upfront during scoping.

How do you handle data confidentiality?

I sign NDAs and data handling agreements before any data transfer. All datasets are stored on encrypted drives and deleted upon project completion unless otherwise agreed. I am experienced with de-identified datasets and HIPAA considerations.

What if reviewers question the statistical methods?

Post-submission support is included. I draft point-by-point statistical responses to reviewer queries and run any additional sensitivity or subgroup analyses they request. My goal is that the statistics never become the reason for rejection.

Can you work with my existing dataset and analysis?

Yes. I can audit existing analyses, clean and restructure datasets, or start from raw data. I work with datasets in any format—SPSS (.sav), Excel, CSV, REDCap exports, or SQL databases.

What is the typical timeline for a project?

Most projects take 3–6 weeks for original research and 4–8 weeks for meta-analyses. Timeline depends on dataset complexity, number of endpoints, and revision cycles. I provide a specific estimate during the scoping call.

How do we communicate during the project?

I provide structured progress updates at each milestone. Communication is primarily via email, with video calls for scoping and major review points. You always know where your project stands.

Do you help with the study design, or only the analysis?

Both. I offer pre-study consultation including sample size calculations, endpoint definitions, and study design review. For ongoing studies, I can join at any stage—from protocol development to post-hoc analysis.

Ready to discuss your project?

I take on a limited number of projects each month to ensure each receives the methodological depth and attention it deserves.
