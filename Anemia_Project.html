Cross-Sectional Prevalence Analysis

Problem

Population-based cross-sectional study estimating prevalence rates with complex sampling design. Stratified population data requiring appropriate weighting and clustering adjustment.

Constraints

Multi-stage sampling requiring design-based analysis. Within-cluster correlation affecting standard errors. Multiple comparison issues across demographic subgroups.

Methodological Approach

Survey-weighted prevalence estimation using complex survey design methods. Design-based confidence intervals accounting for clustering. Rao-Scott chi-square tests for subgroup comparisons.

Output

  • • Weighted prevalence tables by strata
  • • Demographic comparison tables
  • • Visualization of prevalence trends
  • • STROBE-compliant methods text

Outcome

Published. Analysis provided population-level prevalence estimates with appropriate uncertainty quantification. Reviewers did not request statistical revisions.
